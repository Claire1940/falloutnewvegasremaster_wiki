Siding with the NCR represents the "status quo" ending. While they offer safety, they are heavily overextended and plague the wasteland with high taxes. Siding with Mr. House preserves the technological genius of the Old World, but he treats human beings as statistics and demands the destruction of the Brotherhood of Steel. Caesar's Legion provides unparalleled security on trade roads, but their reliance on slavery and absolute cruelty makes them morally repulsive to most players. 

Taking over Vegas yourself via Yes Man is the ultimate wildcard. It is highly recommended for players who want to roleplay as a self-reliant ruler, though the ending slides warn that absolute freedom can lead to temporary chaos in the streets of New Vegas.

<Section icon={Target} title="Yes Man Guide: Fallout New Vegas Should I Take Over Vegas Steps">

If you decide that taking over New Vegas is your preferred destiny, you must follow the Wild Card questline. This path requires you to bypass Mr. House, deal with Benny, and program Yes Man to serve your interests.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate Yes Man">
    Search Benny's room at the Tops Casino on the New Vegas Strip. You will find Yes Man, a modified Securitron programmed to always say yes to any request.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Acquire the Platinum Chip">
    Confront Benny. You can kill him at the Tops Casino or follow him to the Fort, where Caesar holds him captive. Retrieve the Platinum Chip from his body or from Caesar's safe.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Upgrade the Securitron Army">
    Travel to the underground bunker beneath the Fort. Insert the Platinum Chip to upgrade the Securitron software from "Windows XP" to "Windows 10," unlocking their heavy missile launchers and rapid-fire lasers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Resolve Minor Factions">
    Complete the quest 'Wild Card: Side Bets'. You must visit the Boomers, Brotherhood of Steel, Great Khans, and White Glove Society. You can choose to ally with them, ignore them, or wipe them out.
  </Step>
</Steps>

By completing these steps, you prepare the Mojave for a massive transition of power. Yes Man will help you install an override chip at the El Dorado Substation, diverting power directly to the Lucky 38 mainframe and giving you complete control over the city's defenses.

| Quest Name | Objective | Key Reward / Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Wild Card: Side Bets | Meet or ignore minor factions | Secures faction standings for Hoover Dam |
| Wild Card: You and What Army? | Upgrade Securitrons at the Fort | Unlocks Mark II Securitron army upgrades |
| Wild Card: Finishing Touches | Install override chip at El Dorado | Powers up the Lucky 38 network |
| No Gods, No Masters | Complete the Battle of Hoover Dam | Establishes independent New Vegas |

<Callout type="info" title="Securitron Army Power">
Upgrading the Securitron army is highly recommended. Without the Mark II upgrade, your independent ending will result in a much weaker hold over the Mojave, leading to widespread lawlessness.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Minor Factions Alignment Strategy">

To successfully take over Vegas, you must manage your relationships with the minor factions scattered across the wasteland. Unlike other major factions who demand the destruction of groups like the Brotherhood of Steel, Yes Man allows you to decide their fates individually.

The Boomers of Nellis Air Force Base are highly valuable allies. They are heavily armed former Vault dwellers who possess a functional B-52 Bomber. By helping them raise this bomber from Lake Mead, you secure devastating aerial support during the final battle. The Brotherhood of Steel, while isolated in their Hidden Valley bunker, can also provide direct combat support if you complete their internal quests and spare their lives.
