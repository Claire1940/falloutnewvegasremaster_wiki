<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Character Creation Guide">

Creating an optimized character requires careful planning from the moment you distribute your starting points in Doc Mitchell's house. Follow these structured steps to establish a powerful foundation for your chosen playstyle.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Distribute S.P.E.C.I.A.L. Attributes">
    Allocate your starting points carefully. Avoid raising any stat to 10 during character creation, as implants purchased later at the New Vegas Medical Clinic can permanently increase each attribute by 1. Keep Charisma at 1 unless you specifically want companion-focused perks, as its raw combat utility is minimal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Starting Traits">
    Traits offer powerful passive benefits paired with manageable drawbacks. Choose traits that directly complement your endgame weapon choices. For critical-hit builds, Built to Destroy is highly recommended, while Skilled provides a massive early-game boost to all skill lines.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Tag Core Skills">
    Tag three skills to receive an immediate +15 bonus. For combat efficiency, tag your primary offensive skill (Guns, Energy Weapons, or Melee Weapons). For utility, Speech and Lockpick are excellent choices to navigate quests and secure valuable early-game loot.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Plan Your Perk Path">
    Perks are selected every even level. Many high-tier combat perks have strict skill and attribute requirements. Plan ahead to ensure you meet the necessary thresholds (such as reaching 75 Guns for Shotgun Surgeon or 70 Sneak for Silent Running) by the time you reach those levels.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your choice of starting traits significantly influences your early-game progression. Use the table below to identify which traits align best with your planned build.

| Trait Name | Positive Utility | Drawback | Recommended For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Built to Destroy** | +3% Critical Hit Chance | Weapon condition decays 15% faster | Snipers, Critical Laser builds |
| **Good Natured** | +5 to Speech, Medicine, Repair | -5 to Guns, Energy, Melee, Unarmed | Pacifist or Support playstyles |
| **Skilled** | +5 to all character Skills | -10% Experience points gained | All builds (highly versatile) |
| **Trigger Discipline** | +20% Accuracy with guns | 20% slower firing rate | Snipers, Long-range riflemen |
| **Fast Shot** | +20% Firing rate with guns | 20% decreased accuracy | Close-range Shotgunners, SMG builds |

<Callout type="warning" title="Trait Warning">
While the Skilled trait reduces your experience gain by 10%, the level cap in Fallout New Vegas is high enough (especially with DLCs installed) that you will easily reach maximum level regardless. The permanent skill point bonus heavily outweighs the minor experience penalty.
</Callout>
