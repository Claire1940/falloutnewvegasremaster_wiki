<Section icon={Activity} title="Survival and Utility: Hardcore Mode Essentials">

For players engaging in Hardcore mode, the value of **Fallout New Vegas perks** shifts toward weight management and healing efficiency. *Pack Rat* is arguably the most important perk for Hardcore players, as it halves the weight of all items weighing 2 lbs or less, including ammunition.

Maintenance is another hurdle. *Jury Rigging* allows you to repair weapons and armor using similar items rather than identical ones. This means a high-end Power Armor can be repaired using basic Metal Armor found on raiders, saving thousands of caps in repair costs.

<Callout type="success" title="Hardcore Tip: Pack Rat">
On Hardcore mode, ammo has weight. **Pack Rat** reduces the weight of thousands of rounds of 5.56mm or Microfusion Cells, allowing you to carry a much wider arsenal without becoming overencumbered.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Weight Management">
    Take **Pack Rat** at level 8 (requires 70 Barter). This is the single biggest quality-of-life improvement for inventory management.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Optimize Repair Efficiency">
    Reach 90 Repair by level 14 to unlock **Jury Rigging**. This effectively removes the need for weapon repair kits or expensive vendors.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enhance Healing Sources">
    Consider **Fast Metabolism** or **Them's Good Eating** (DLC) to ensure your healing items provide enough HP per second to counter combat damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Zap} title="DLC Power Spikes: Dead Money to Lonesome Road">

The expansion packs introduce some of the most specialized **Fallout New Vegas perks** in the game. From the stealth-focused bonuses of *Dead Money* to the raw power of *Old World Blues*, these perks often require high skill investments but offer game-changing mechanics.

*Implant GRX* is the pinnacle of DLC perks. It grants the player a non-addictive "Turbo" effect that slows down time, making it impossible for enemies to react. When combined with the *Chemist* perk, the duration of this effect is doubled, allowing you to clear entire rooms before a single bullet hits you.

<Section icon={Target} title="The Blacklist: Perks You Should Never Take">

Not all **Fallout New Vegas perks** are created equal. Some are bugged, while others provide bonuses so negligible they aren't worth the slot. Understanding what to avoid is just as important as knowing what to pick. For instance, *In Shining Armor* is famously bugged—it provides zero benefit because the game fails to recognize "reflective" armor types correctly.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid: Here and Now">
The perk **Here and Now** grants an instant level up. However, since the level cap is fixed (50 with all DLCs), taking this perk means you effectively lose one perk slot forever in exchange for something you would have achieved through 15 minutes of gameplay.
</Callout>

| Avoid Perk | Reason for Failure | Better Alternative |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **In Shining Armor** | Code is bugged; provides 0 bonus | **Toughness** (+3 DT) |
| **Here and Now** | Waste of a permanent perk slot | Any combat or utility perk |
| **Swift Learner** | XP is infinite; perk slots are limited | **Comprehension** |
| **Lead Belly** | Radiation from food is a minor issue | **Rad Child** (Turns rads into healing) |
| **Computer Whiz** | Just exit the terminal before the last try | **Science** skill points |

</Section>
