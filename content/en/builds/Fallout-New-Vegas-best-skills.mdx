<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The 'Skilled' Trait Exploit for Maximum Points">

When Doc Mitchell asks you to pick your traits, the "Skilled" trait is arguably the most powerful option available. It grants a permanent +5 bonus to every single skill in the game. While it carries a -10% experience penalty, this is negligible in the long run as there is more than enough XP in the Mojave to reach level 50.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Selection">
    Pick the **Skilled** trait at Doc Mitchell's house. This gives you an immediate +5 to all skills.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Goodsprings Respec">
    Complete the tutorial and head toward the Goodsprings border. When the prompt appears to rebuild your character, select **Skilled** again. The bonuses stack, giving you a total of +10 to every skill.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Old World Blues Reset">
    Once you reach the Old World Blues DLC, use the Auto-Doc in the Sink to reset your traits one last time. Pick **Skilled** again for a permanent +15 bonus to all skills.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Trait | Positive Effect | Negative Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Skilled** | +5 to all skills | -10% Experience gain |
| **Good Natured** | +5 to non-combat skills | -10 to combat skills |
| **Small Frame** | +1 Agility | Limbs break more easily |
| **Built to Destroy** | +3% Critical Hit chance | Equipment decays 15% faster |

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Bug Warning">
The XP penalty from the 'Skilled' trait does not stack, even though the skill point bonus does. This makes it an essential pick for any build aiming for the **Fallout New Vegas best skills** layout.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Leveling Strategy and Permanent Dead Money Perks">

A common mistake players make is leaving Goodsprings at level 1. To truly optimize your character, you should reach level 2 before crossing the border. This allows you to pick a permanent perk that stays with you even after you "rebuild" your character at the border.

The **Fallout New Vegas best skills** are often complemented by perks from the Dead Money DLC. Specifically, the perk **Light Touch** is incredibly powerful for critical hit builds.

| Perk | Requirement | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light Touch** | Agility 6, Repair 45 | +5% Crit Chance, enemies -25% Crit Chance |
| **Old World Gourmet** | Endurance 6, Survival 45 | +25% Health from snacks, 50% Rad resistance from food |
| **Junk Rounds** | Luck 6, Repair 45 | Craft ammunition using scrap metal and tin cans |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Reach Level 2">
    Kill geckos at the springs, hack the safe in the schoolhouse, and complete Sunny Smiles' tutorial to hit level 2.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Dead Money Perk">
    Pick **Light Touch** or **Old World Gourmet**. These perks are unique because they are not removed during the character rebuild at the Goodsprings border.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Rebuild Character">
    Step across the border, keep your level 2 perk, and then re-assign your SPECIAL and Skills to your permanent endgame build.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Build Synergy">
Combining **Light Touch** with the **Skilled** trait and high **Luck** creates a character that can land critical hits almost constantly, even with basic weapons like the Varmint Rifle.
</Callout>

</Section>

For players who want a "broken" start, there is an infinite XP loop involving the quest "Run Goodsprings Run." This exploit allows you to reach level 50 before ever seeing the New Vegas strip, though it can trivialize the game's challenge.

**How the Chet Exploit Works:**
1. Side with Joe Cobb and kill Ringo.
2. Tell Joe Cobb that Ringo is dead.
3. Joe Cobb will tell you to shake down Chet at the General Store.
4. Go to Chet and use the **Barter** or **Speech** check (requires 25).
5. Instead of finishing the quest, keep repeating the dialogue option.
