<Section icon={Activity} title="Technical Performance and Visual Fidelity">

When launching **Fallout New Vegas ps5**, the first thing players will notice is the "jagged" nature of the visuals. Because there is no modern anti-aliasing (AA) applied to the stream, the desert landscapes and character models can appear very rough on modern high-definition displays. The environment often suffers from low-resolution textures, particularly on distant rock formations and buildings like the Lucky 38.

| Connectivity Metric | Minimum Requirement | Recommended for PS5 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Download Speed** | 15 Mbps | 100+ Mbps |
| **Upload Speed** | 5 Mbps | 30+ Mbps |
| **Connection Type** | Wi-Fi (2.4GHz) | Wired Ethernet / 5GHz |
| **Latency (Ping)** | < 50ms | < 20ms |

The performance is locked at 30 frames per second, which can feel sluggish compared to modern 60 FPS or 120 FPS titles. However, the VATS (Vault-Tec Assisted Targeting System) helps mitigate some of the frustration associated with aiming in a lower-framerate environment. Players should expect occasional "chugging" or frame drops during intense combat encounters or when entering densely populated areas like the New Vegas Strip.

<Callout type="tip" title="Network Optimization">
For the best experience, always use a **wired Ethernet connection**. Cloud streaming is extremely sensitive to packet loss, which is common over standard Wi-Fi.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Navigating the Mojave: A Step-by-Step Start">

The journey of the Courier begins with a bullet to the head and a shallow grave. Once you are patched up by Doc Mitchell, your primary goal is to track down the men who tried to kill you and recover the Platinum Chip. Following the main road south from Goodsprings is the safest route for new players, as heading straight north toward Vegas will lead you directly into a nest of Deathclaws.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Best Practices for Streamed Gameplay">

Because you are playing a legacy title via the cloud, technical stability is your greatest enemy. The PS3 version of the game was notoriously prone to crashing, and while the PS5 hardware is powerful, the underlying software remains fragile. Implementing a rigorous saving strategy is the only way to ensure you don't lose hours of progress to a server disconnect or a random game freeze.

| Save Type | Frequency | Recommended Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quick Save** | Every 5-10 mins | Before entering new buildings |
| **Manual Save** | Every 30 mins | After completing major quests |
| **Auto Save** | On Transition | Enabled by default (don't rely on it) |
| **Rotation** | 3-5 Slots | Always keep multiple rolling saves |

Another limitation of the **Fallout New Vegas ps5** experience is the lack of modern Quality of Life (QoL) features. There is no sprint button, which makes traversing the vast desert feel slow. Additionally, the low resolution can make it difficult to spot landmines or small items on the ground. Use VATS frequently not just for combat, but as a "radar" to detect nearby enemies or traps that might be obscured by the blurry stream.

<Callout type="warning" title="Save Corruption">
Avoid relying solely on a single save file. Legacy Bethesda titles can occasionally suffer from save bloat or corruption. Maintaining at least three separate manual save slots is highly recommended.
</Callout>

</Section>
