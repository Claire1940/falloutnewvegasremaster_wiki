<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Upgraded Technology: Creation Engine 3">

One of the most significant revelations from the announcement is that the **Fallout New Vegas remaster** and other upcoming titles will be built on Creation Engine 3. This shared technology platform represents the next evolution of Bethesda's proprietary engine, which has been iteratively developed since the launch of Starfield. By aligning all their major RPGs under a single engine, Bethesda aims to streamline asset sharing, physics simulations, and modding tools across their development teams.

Transitioning a classic like New Vegas to a modern engine is no small feat. The original game, developed by Obsidian and released in 2010, ran on the highly unstable Gamebryo engine. This resulted in frequent crashes, memory leaks, and severe performance bottlenecks that fans had to patch themselves over the years. A shift to Creation Engine 3 promises to resolve these legacy architectural issues, offering native 64-bit support, improved memory allocation, and modern rendering pipelines.

<Callout type="info" title="Modding Community Impact">
The transition to Creation Engine 3 means that original mods will not be compatible. Modders will need to rebuild their creations using the updated Creation Kit tools.
</Callout>
