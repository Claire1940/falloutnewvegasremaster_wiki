<Section icon={Map} title="The Legend of Fallout New Vegas Joshua Graham">

Unraveling the legacy of **Fallout New Vegas Joshua Graham** requires journeying into the heart of Zion Canyon in the *Honest Hearts* DLC. Known throughout the Mojave Wasteland as "The Burned Man," Graham is one of the most compelling and complex figures in the franchise. Once the brutal co-founder and first Legate of Caesar's Legion, his failure at the First Battle of Hoover Dam led to a horrific punishment: he was covered in pitch, set on fire, and cast into the depths of the Grand Canyon.

Against all odds, Graham survived. Returning to his roots among the New Canaanites, he eventually became the war chief of the Dead Horses tribe in Zion. His character represents a volatile mix of deep religious devotion, profound remorse, and an underlying capacity for extreme violence. When players encounter him in Angel Cave, they are introduced to a leader preparing to wage a merciless war of survival against the invading White Legs tribe.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Signature Gear and Weapon Analysis">

Joshua Graham is defined by his iconic gear, which players can obtain upon completing the *Honest Hearts* storyline. His equipment is not merely cosmetic; it forms the backbone of some of the strongest critical-hit builds in the game.

His armor is a modified police tactical vest worn over dirty bandages, offering an exceptional balance of protection and utility. Unlike heavy power armor, this light armor set allows players to retain high movement speed while gaining a significant boost to critical hit chance.

| Armor Name | DT | Weight | Effects | Classification |
| Joshua Graham's Armor | 15 | 8 | Crit Chance +3% | Light |
| Elite Riot Gear | 22 | 23 | Guns +10, Charisma +1, Crit Chance +5% | Heavy |
| Sierra Madre Armor, Reinforced | 18 | 20 | Agility +1 | Medium |

His unique weapon, *A Light Shining in Darkness*, is a customized Colt Model 1911 (.45 Auto pistol) featuring a trench-style sight and a snakeskin grip. It boasts incredibly high damage per second (DPS) due to its rapid fire rate and high critical hit multiplier.

| Weapon Name | Damage | DPS | Crit Multiplier | Ammo Type |
| A Light Shining in Darkness | 33 | 165 | 2.0x | .45 Auto |
| Standard .45 Auto Pistol | 28 | 97 | 1.0x | .45 Auto |
| Li'l Devil | 45 | 139 | 2.0x | 12.7mm |

<Section icon={Target} title="Quest Walkthrough and Ending Choices">

The climax of the *Honest Hearts* DLC centers around a profound moral conflict between Joshua Graham and the missionary Daniel. The survival of the Sorrows and Dead Horses tribes hangs in the balance, and your decisions will permanently alter the personality of these communities.

| Ending Choice | Faction Outcome | Joshua's Fate | Zion's Future |
| Crush the White Legs | Sorrows become warlike | Joshua finds peace (if spared) | Zion remains under Dead Horse control |
| Flee Zion | Sorrows remain peaceful | Joshua is filled with regret | Zion is lost to the White Legs |
| Executing Salt-Upon-Wounds | Sorrows embrace brutality | Joshua becomes cold and ruthless | White Legs are completely annihilated |

To navigate the final sequence and achieve the most favorable outcome for Joshua's redemption, follow these precise steps.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Complete the Preparations">
    Gather all necessary supplies for the tribes and complete the preliminary quests given by Joshua and Daniel in Zion Canyon.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Choose the Assault Path">
    When speaking with Daniel, agree to support Joshua's plan to stand and fight the White Legs rather than fleeing the valley.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Confront Salt-Upon-Wounds">
    Fight your way through the White Leg camp alongside Joshua until you corner their leader, Salt-Upon-Wounds.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Intervene in the Dialogue">
    Pass a Speech check (level 90) or choose the dialogue options that encourage mercy. Convince Joshua to spare Salt-Upon-Wounds or fight him in a fair trial by combat.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Moral Trap">
If you allow Joshua to execute Salt-Upon-Wounds in cold blood, the ending slides reveal that he remains consumed by his past brutality, and the Sorrows tribe adopts a harsh, unforgiving militarism. Sparing the warlord leads to a much more peaceful future.
</Callout>

</Section>
