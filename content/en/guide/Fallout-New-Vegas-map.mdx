<Section icon={Target} title="Technical Size and Distance Calculations">

When discussing the **Fallout New Vegas map**, it is important to distinguish between the "playable area" and the "rendered area." While the playable Mojave is roughly 75.4 square kilometers, the game engine renders low-resolution terrain that extends significantly further.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Internal Grid System">
    The game divides the world into cells. The core "active" world consists of a square grid where most NPCs and quests are located.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="LOD Rendering">
    Low-resolution (Level of Detail) terrain is generated far beyond the physical boundaries to create the illusion of a vast horizon.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Teleport Trigger">
    If a player bypasses boundaries (via mods or console), they eventually reach a coordinate threshold that teleports them back to the center of the map.
  </Step>
</Steps>

In terms of pure numbers, the base map is approximately 8.68 km on each side. If we account for the entire rendered terrain—the "background" mountains and plains—the theoretical size balloons to over 77,000 square kilometers, which is comparable to the size of a small country like Serbia.

| Metric | Value (Approximate) |
|:---|:---|
| **Playable Width** | 8.68 km |
| **Playable Length** | 8.68 km |
| **Total Playable Area** | 75.4 sq km |
| **Theoretical Rendered Area** | 77,000+ sq km |

<Callout type="info" title="Calculation Method">
These measurements are derived from compass pixel analysis and trigonometric scaling based on movement speed at 100% vs 4000% velocity.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Exploration Secrets Beyond the Map">

For those who venture beyond the intended path on the **Fallout New Vegas map**, the world becomes increasingly surreal. The further you travel from the Vegas Strip, the more the game's systems begin to break down.

- **The Void**: Eventually, the ground stops rendering entirely, leaving only the skybox and the water plane.
- **Radiated Swimming**: The western ocean is not just a backdrop; it is a fully interactive, albeit lethal, body of water.
- **Terrain Degradation**: Rocks and trees stop appearing first, followed by high-quality ground textures, leaving only a "blobby" terrain mesh.
- **The Loop**: Reaching the absolute edge of the coordinate system doesn't crash the game; it simply resets your position to a predefined "safe" coordinate near the map's center.

| Stage of Out-of-Bounds | Visuals | Player Experience |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Zone 1: Immediate** | Low-res hills | Invisible walls/Physical blocks |
| **Zone 2: Intermediate** | Terrain only | Extreme radiation / No collision |
| **Zone 3: Extreme** | Water plane | Endless swimming / Teleport risk |

<Callout type="tip" title="Console Exploration">
If you wish to see these areas yourself, open the console with the tilde (~) key and type `tcl`. This enables "No Clip" mode, allowing you to fly through the physical barriers and explore the rendered horizon.
</Callout>
