<Section icon={Shield} title="Dead Money Survival Strategies">

Dead Money shifts the game from an action-RPG into a tense survival-horror experience. Upon entering the Sierra Madre bunker, the receptionist gas knocks you unconscious, and you awaken with all your gear confiscated. You must rely on scavenged items, a basic holographic rifle, and your wits to survive the toxic red cloud and the mysterious ghost people roaming the villa.

The primary environmental hazards are the toxic red cloud, which drains health over time, and the security holograms that patrol key areas. Additionally, your collar will beep rapidly when near active radios or speakers. If you do not destroy or disable the signal source within a few seconds, your collar will detonate, resulting in an instant game over.

| Hazard Type | Primary Threat | Countermeasure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Red Cloud** | Constant health degeneration | Avoid low-lying areas, use stimpaks, find clean routes |
| **Speakers & Radios** | Explodes player collar | Shoot blinking red speakers, turn off radios via terminals |
| **Ghost People** | High melee damage, revive unless dismembered | Cut off limbs after knocking down, use bear trap fists |
| **Holograms** | Invincible security units | Locate and disable the physical projector emitters nearby |

To navigate the villa, you must recruit three unique companions locked in various districts. Each companion provides a passive perk that helps mitigate the Sierra Madre's harsh environment.

| Companion | Recruitment Location | Unique Utility Perk | Best Combat Style |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dog / God** | Police Station | Immune to traps, eats ghost people | Close Quarters Melee |
| **Dean Domino** | Residential District | Increases toxic cloud resistance | Ranged Pistol Support |
| **Christine** | Clinic Basement | Disables security holograms | Stealth & Hacking |

<Callout type="warning" title="Collar Detonation">
Always listen for the rapid beeping of your collar. Locate and destroy the blinking red radio transmitters immediately using melee attacks or small arms to prevent instant death.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Honest Hearts Exploration & Zion Quests">

Honest Hearts takes you to the beautiful, untouched wilderness of Zion National Park in Utah. After joining the Happy Trails Expedition, your caravan is quickly ambushed by the hostile White Legs tribe. From there, you are swept into a conflict between the local tribes, led by the missionary Daniel and the legendary, battle-scarred Joshua Graham, also known as the Burned Man.

Zion is rich in natural resources, unique wildlife like Yao Guai, and pre-war ruins. Unlike other expansions, Honest Hearts allows you to keep most of your gear, though you must adhere to a strict weight limit when first entering the canyon.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Join the Caravan">
    Meet Jed Masterson at the Northern Passage in the Mojave. Trim your inventory to meet the strict weight limits before starting the journey.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Survive the Ambush">
    Defend against the initial White Legs assault. Avoid shooting Follows-Chalk when he appears on the ridge, as doing so will fail the main questline.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Gather Sacred Supplies">
    Explore the canyon to collect survival gear, medical kits, and walkie-talkies from pre-war ruins, fishing lodges, and ranger stations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Decide Zion's Fate">
    Choose between helping Daniel evacuate the tribes to safety or assisting Joshua Graham in eliminating the White Legs threat permanently.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The rewards for completing Honest Hearts are highly prized, especially for critical-hit and light armor builds. Joshua Graham's armor and unique pistol are among the best gear pieces in the entire game.

| Item Name | Weapon/Armor Type | Primary Stat | Key Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A Light Shining in Darkness** | Pistol (.45 Auto) | 33 Damage | High critical hit chance, fast reload |
| **Joshua Graham's Armor** | Light Armor | 15 DT | Boosts critical hit chance by 3% |
| **Compliance Regulator** | Laser Pistol | 15 Damage | Freezes targets on critical hits |
| **Desert Ranger Armor** | Medium Armor | 22 DT | High durability, no faction alignment |

<Callout type="tip" title="Inventory Management">
Before starting Honest Hearts, invest points into the Pack Rat or Strong Back perks to maximize the amount of unique loot you can carry back to the Mojave.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Old World Blues Science & Upgrades">

Old World Blues transports you to the pre-war research center of Big Mountain, often called Big MT. You wake up to find your brain, spine, and heart have been replaced with high-tech robotic equivalents. To retrieve your original organs, you must cooperate with the eccentric robotic scientists of the Think Tank and stop the rogue Dr. Mobius.

This expansion functions as a large, rewarding open-world sandbox. It features a central player home called the Sink, which can be upgraded with various talking appliances that offer unique services.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Lonesome Road Final Showdown">

Lonesome Road brings the Courier's journey to a dramatic, linear conclusion. Guided by the mysterious Courier Ulysses, you travel deep into the Divide, a ruined landscape torn apart by earthquakes, storms, and active nuclear warheads. Along the way, you are accompanied by a prototype eyebot named ED-E, who provides combat support and utility upgrades.

To navigate the Divide, you must use a Laser Detonator to detonate active nuclear warheads scattered across the map. These explosions clear paths, open secret areas, and deal massive damage to nearby enemies like Marked Men and Tunnelers.

| Enemy Type | Primary Threat | Best Weapon Choice | Tactical Advice |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Marked Men** | High-tier firearms, group tactics | Sniper rifles, explosives | Take them out from distance |
| **Tunnelers** | Fast melee attacks, swarm behavior | Grenade launchers, flare guns | Use flares to blind and scatter |
| **Deathclaws** | Extreme damage, high speed | Anti-Materiel Rifle (50 MG) | Target legs to cripple movement |

At the end of the campaign, you face Ulysses and must make a critical choice regarding the nuclear missiles targeting the Mojave. Your choice significantly impacts your faction reputation and unlocks unique high-tier armor sets.

| Final Choice | Faction Reputation Impact | Unique Reward | Best Build Match |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nuke NCR** | Vilified by NCR | Armor of the 87th Tribe | Melee & Survival |
| **Nuke Legion** | Vilified by Legion | Scorched Sierra Power Armor | Heavy Weapons |
| **Nuke Both** | Vilified by Both | Access to both secret zones | Completionist |
| **Cancel Launch** | Famous with Followers | Brotherhood of Steel Fame | Good Karma |

<Callout type="warning" title="Tunneler Threat">
Tunnelers travel in packs and deal massive melee damage. Use flare guns or flashbangs to blind them, or rely on high-yield explosives to control the crowd.
</Callout>
