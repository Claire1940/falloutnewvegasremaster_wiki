<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Protection: Damage Threshold Mechanics">

Understanding armor in Fallout New Vegas requires mastering the **Damage Threshold (DT)** system. Unlike the percentage-based Damage Resistance (DR) found in other RPGs, DT subtracts a flat value from incoming damage. If an enemy hits you for 25 damage and you have 20 DT, you only take 5 damage. However, every attack will always deal at least 20% of its original damage (the "bleed-through" mechanic), regardless of how high your DT is.

Choosing the right armor depends heavily on your character's perks. For example, the **Light Touch** perk is only active while wearing light armor, providing a significant boost to critical hit chance and reducing enemy critical hit chance against you.

| Armor Category | Typical DT Range | Best Usage | Key Perk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light** | 2 - 12 | Stealth, Sniping, Speed | Light Touch |
| **Medium** | 13 - 22 | General Combat, Quests | Jury Rigging |
| **Heavy** | 20 - 28 | Tanking, High-End Bosses | Power Armor Training |

<Callout type="info" title="DT vs. DR">
While New Vegas focuses on DT, some consumables like Med-X still provide DR. Combining high DT armor with DR-boosting drugs makes your character nearly invincible against high-frequency, low-damage weapons like SMGs.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Top-Tier Power Armor and How to Get It">

Power Armor represents the pinnacle of pre-war technology. In the Mojave, these suits are rare and require specialized training to operate. You can obtain this training by either progressing through the **Brotherhood of Steel** questline at Hidden Valley or by completing the **For Auld Lang Syne** quest with Arcade Gannon to reunite the Enclave Remnants.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate the Brotherhood">
    Travel to Hidden Valley and locate the bunker. You will need to complete several tasks for Elder McNamara or Head Paladin Hardin to gain entry.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Complete Training Quests">
    Follow the "Still in the Dark" questline. Upon completion, you will be granted the Power Armor Training perk and a suit of T-45d Power Armor.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Hunt for the Remnants">
    Recruit Arcade Gannon and trigger his personal quest. This leads to the Remnants Power Armor, the strongest defensive set in the game.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Power Armor Set | DT | Weight | Special Bonuses |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **T-45d Power Armor** | 22 | 45 | +2 STR, -2 AGI, +10 Rad Res |
| **T-51b Power Armor** | 25 | 40 | +1 STR, +25 Rad Res |

<Callout type="warning" title="Training Required">
You cannot equip Power Armor simply by looting it. Without the Power Armor Training perk, the armor will remain in your inventory as heavy dead weight. Plan your faction alliances carefully to unlock this early.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Maintenance and the Jury Rigging Perk">

One of the biggest challenges with high-end Fallout New Vegas armor is the cost of maintenance. A suit of Ranger Combat Armor can cost upwards of 15,000 caps to repair at a vendor if it falls into poor condition. For most players, this is an unsustainable drain on resources.

The solution lies in the **Jury Rigging** perk, which is widely considered the most powerful utility perk in the game. It allows you to repair items with other items of the same general category rather than the exact same model.

| Repair Method | Cost | Requirements | Efficiency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vendor Repair** | Very High | Caps only | Instant but expensive |
| **Direct Repair** | Free | Identical Armor set | Requires rare duplicates |
| **Weapon Repair Kits** | Low | Crafting materials | Weapons only, no armor |
| **Jury Rigging** | Free | 90 Repair, Level 14 | Use common gear to fix rare gear |

With Jury Rigging, you can fix your rare Ranger Combat Armor (Medium) using a common set of Legion Centurion armor or even standard Combat Armor found on Mercenaries. This ensures your best gear stays at 100% condition without spending a single cap.

<Callout type="tip" title="Quick Repair Hack">
Keep a "repair suit" in your inventory—any armor with a +5 or +10 Repair bonus—to help hit the 90-point threshold for Jury Rigging earlier in your playthrough.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Faction Armor and Stealth Mechanics">

Wearing faction armor is a double-edged sword. While it allows you to infiltrate enemy territory, it also strips away your personal reputation and replaces it with the faction's standing. If you wear Great Khan armor into an NCR camp, you will be shot on sight, regardless of how many quests you have done for the Republic.

  <Card title="Risks of Faction Gear">

  </Card>

| **Great Khans** | Simple/Tough | NCR |
| **Brotherhood** | Power Armor | NCR (usually), Caesar's Legion |
