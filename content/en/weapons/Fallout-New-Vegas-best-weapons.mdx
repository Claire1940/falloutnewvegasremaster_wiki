<Section icon={Target} title="Long-Range Precision and Sniper Rifles">

Sniping remains the safest way to clear out a Caesar's Legion camp or a nest of Deathclaws. The **Fallout New Vegas best weapons** for long-range engagement are those that support silencers and high-magnification scopes. Accuracy and critical damage are the two most important stats for these builds.

The **Gobi Campaign Scout Rifle** is a fan favorite for its high critical hit chance and desert camouflage. However, for those with the *Old World Blues* DLC, **Christine's CoS Silencer Rifle** is often considered the superior choice due to its built-in suppressor and massive critical damage bonus.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Acquire High Lockpick">
    Most elite sniper rifles, like the **Gobi Campaign Scout Rifle**, are locked behind Level 100 (Very Hard) locks. Ensure your Lockpick skill is maxed early.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Visit the Sniper's Nest">
    Locate the Sniper's Nest overlooking Cottonwood Cove to find the Gobi rifle in a locked footlocker.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Secure Ammo Supply">
    Elite snipers use .308 or .50 MG rounds. Visit the **Great Khan Armorer** or **Bardon at Hoover Dam** for the best bulk prices.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Apply Perks">
    Take the **Better Criticals** and **Ratslayer** perks to maximize the one-shot potential of your suppressed weapons.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Ammo Management">
The **Anti-Materiel Rifle** (and its unique GRA mods) deals the most damage but has a very slow fire rate. Always carry a sidearm like **That Gun** or **Lucky** for close-range emergencies.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Energy Weapons and High-Tech Destruction">

Energy weapons offer some of the most visually impressive ways to dispatch foes. From disintegrating targets into ash piles to shocking them with orbital lasers, this category is versatile. The **Fallout New Vegas best weapons** for energy builds often depend on whether you chose the **Wild Wasteland** trait at the start of the game.

If you have Wild Wasteland, you can obtain the **Alien Blaster**, which has a 100% critical hit chance but limited ammunition. Without the trait, you get the **YCS/186**, a Gauss rifle that hits like a freight train and uses standard Microfusion Cells.

| Energy Weapon | Ammo Type | Stat Requirement | Special Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Alien Blaster** | Alien Power Cell | 0 Energy Weapons | 100% Critical Chance |
| **YCS/186** | Microfusion Cell | 75 Energy Weapons | High Knockback |
| **Holorifle** | Microfusion Cell | 50 Energy Weapons | Damage over time effect |
| **Pew Pew** | Energy Cell | 75 Energy Weapons | Fires 2 shots at once |
| **Sprtel-Wood 9700** | electron Charge Pack | 100 Energy Weapons | High rate of fire gatling |

<Callout type="info" title="DLC Powerhouse">
The **Holorifle**, obtained at the start of the *Dead Money* DLC, is one of the most efficient energy weapons in the game. It functions like a pump-action shotgun but fires concentrated energy slugs that deal lingering damage.
</Callout>
